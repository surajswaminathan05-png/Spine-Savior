SPINE SAVIOR: A WEARABLE MULTI-SENSOR BIOFEEDBACK SYSTEM FOR REAL-TIME SPINAL POSTURE MONITORING, ON-DEVICE MACHINE LEARNING CLASSIFICATION, AND HAPTIC CORRECTIVE FEEDBACK

Poor posture affects approximately 80% of the population and is a leading cause of chronic back pain. Existing commercial devices use only a single tilt sensor and cannot identify which spinal region is misaligned. Spine Savior addresses this limitation with a wearable system that independently monitors the cervical (neck), thoracic (upper back), and lumbar (lower back) regions of the spine using three Bosch BNO055 9-degree-of-freedom inertial measurement units, each combining an accelerometer, gyroscope, and magnetometer with onboard sensor fusion. A Texas Instruments TCA9548A I2C multiplexer resolves address conflicts between the identical sensors, and an Arduino Nano 33 BLE microcontroller polls all three at 60 Hz, transmitting orientation data wirelessly via Bluetooth Low Energy.

A browser-based application receives the data and renders a biomechanically constrained 3D spinal model using Three.js, applying clinically sourced per-vertebra range-of-motion limits and Gaussian falloff blending across all 24 articulating vertebrae. A personalized 1D Convolutional Neural Network (~69,500 parameters), trained on-device via TensorFlow.js on each user's unique body, classifies five posture types: Normal, Forward Head, Slouching, Lordosis, and Lateral Tilt. The classifier achieves over 90% validation accuracy on a 20% held-out test set.

When poor posture is detected, two feedback mechanisms activate: Google Gemini Nano generates context-aware coaching tips locally, and dual haptic vibration motors deliver tactile alerts at the affected spinal region. The entire system operates with zero cloud dependency, ensuring complete biometric data privacy.
